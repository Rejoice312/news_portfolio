Ikwerre Teachers Undergo New Professional Development Training

Date: January 13, 2026   |   Time: 12:45 PM

Location: Ikwerre, Rivers State

By: Rejoice Chinwendu



In a strategic effort to elevate the standard of teaching and learning across public schools, the Rivers State Ministry of Education has commenced a comprehensive professional development and welfare program for teachers in Ikwerre Local Government Area. The initiative is designed to equip educators with modern teaching methodologies, digital skills, and updated curriculum knowledge.

The program features a series of workshops, seminars, and hands-on sessions facilitated by experienced education consultants and university lecturers. Core focus areas include learner-centered teaching strategies, classroom management, digital literacy, and effective assessment techniques. The training also addresses teacher welfare, highlighting motivation, stress management, and professional growth.

Speaking at the opening ceremony in Isiokpo, the Commissioner for Education, Professor Ngozi Okoro, emphasized the importance of continuous teacher development. She noted that well-equipped and motivated teachers are essential for producing students who can think critically and compete academically.

Many teachers welcomed the initiative, describing it as timely and impactful. Participants expressed enthusiasm about incorporating technology into their lessons and adopting more engaging teaching approaches that enhance student understanding.

The program also promotes collaboration among teachers, creating a platform for sharing best practices and addressing common challenges in the classroom. Students are expected to benefit from more dynamic, interactive, and effective teaching methods.

Beyond training, the Ministry of Education announced plans to review teacher incentives, improve working conditions, and provide clearer career advancement pathways. This holistic approach aims to attract and retain skilled educators across Rivers State.
