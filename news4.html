Degema Youth Benefit from New Vocational Skills Scholarship

Date: January 13, 2026   |   Time: 10:50 AM

Location: Degema, Rivers State

By: Rejoice Chinwendu

In a decisive move to promote youth empowerment and economic sustainability, the Rivers State Government has launched a vocational skills scholarship scheme targeting indigent and talented youths in Degema Local Government Area. The initiative is designed to equip beneficiaries with practical, employable skills that support self-reliance and entrepreneurship.

The scholarship provides full sponsorship for intensive training in various trades, including electrical installation, fashion design, carpentry, plumbing, catering, ICT repairs, and digital marketing. Beneficiaries were selected based on aptitude, interest, and financial need, with training conducted at accredited vocational centers.

Speaking at the launch ceremony in Degema, the Special Adviser to the Governor on Youth Empowerment, Hon. Kenneth Adoki, stressed the importance of vocational education in addressing unemployment. He noted that skill acquisition creates alternative pathways to economic stability beyond conventional academic routes.

Beneficiaries expressed gratitude for the opportunity, describing the program as life-changing. Many noted that the scholarship removed financial barriers that had previously prevented them from acquiring professional skills.

Community leaders and local government officials welcomed the initiative, highlighting its potential to stimulate economic growth and reduce youth restiveness in riverine communities. Mentorship and business support components have also been integrated to ensure long-term success for participants.

The Degema vocational scholarship scheme is expected to serve as a model for similar youth-focused programs across Rivers State, reinforcing the government’s commitment to inclusive development and workforce empowerment.
