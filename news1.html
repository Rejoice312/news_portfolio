State Expands School Feeding Program to Ahoada East

Date: January 13, 2026   |   Time: 01:30 PM

Location: Ahoada East, Rivers State

By: Rejoice Chinwendu



In a significant move aimed at improving child nutrition, boosting school enrollment, and enhancing learning outcomes, the Rivers State Government has officially expanded its robust school feeding program to include public primary schools in Ahoada East Local Government Area. This initiative, which provides free, nutritious meals to pupils during school hours, is part of a broader commitment to ensuring that no child is left behind in the pursuit of quality education, especially those from vulnerable backgrounds.

The program's expansion into Ahoada East comes after successful implementation in several other LGAs, where it has demonstrably led to increased attendance rates and improved concentration among students. By providing a daily meal, the state government is directly addressing a fundamental barrier to education, allowing pupils to focus on their studies without the distraction of hunger. The meals are carefully planned to be balanced and locally sourced, supporting local farmers and economies.

Speaking during the launch ceremony, the Commissioner for Primary Education, Dr. Helen Davies, emphasized the long-term benefits of the initiative. She described the program as an investment in children’s health, future, and the overall development of Rivers State.

Parents and guardians in Ahoada East have welcomed the initiative with relief, noting that the program eases financial burdens while encouraging consistent school attendance. Teachers have also reported improved energy levels, participation, and attentiveness among pupils since the commencement of the feeding program.

Community leaders have commended the government’s foresight, describing the initiative as a critical step toward tackling child malnutrition and strengthening foundational education. Local monitoring committees have been established to ensure transparency, quality, and sustainability of the program.

With the successful rollout in Ahoada East, the Rivers State Government reaffirmed its commitment to expanding the school feeding program across more communities, reinforcing education as a cornerstone for social and economic development.
