Modern Libraries and Labs Unveiled in Oyigbo Schools

Date: January 13, 2026   |   Time: 11:55 AM

Location: Oyigbo, Rivers State

By: Rejoice Chinwendu

Students in public secondary schools across Oyigbo Local Government Area of Rivers State are set to experience a new phase of academic advancement following the commissioning of newly renovated and fully equipped modern libraries and science laboratories in selected schools. The initiative aims to strengthen learning outcomes by improving access to quality educational resources.

The facilities feature up-to-date textbooks, reference materials, digital learning tools, and comfortable reading spaces. The newly installed science laboratories are equipped with modern apparatus for physics, chemistry, and biology practicals, addressing long-standing infrastructure challenges in public schools within the area.

Speaking during the unveiling ceremony at Government Secondary School, Oyigbo, the Commissioner for Works and Infrastructure, Engr. David Enyinda, reaffirmed the state government’s commitment to providing a conducive environment for learning and scientific exploration. He described the facilities as gateways to innovation and discovery.

Teachers and school administrators expressed optimism that the upgraded facilities would significantly enhance students’ academic performance and practical understanding. The availability of functional laboratories is expected to improve outcomes in science-related subjects and better prepare students for external examinations.

Students also welcomed the development, noting that hands-on experiments would make learning more engaging and meaningful. Parents commended the government for investing in infrastructure that supports long-term educational growth.

The Rivers State Government urged school authorities and community leaders to ensure proper maintenance and security of the facilities. Plans are underway to extend similar projects to other local government areas as part of a broader education reform strategy.
